(attempting to) repair my x201

i recently picked up my dream laptop, the lenovo x201, for a decent price.

admittedly its not really what id consider my dream laptop anymore after having to deal with the built-in intel spyware and overheating issues, but alas

but i needed a laptop for my classes, and after seeing one for a reasonable price, i decided why not.

i broke it less than a week after buying it lol

accidently knocked it off my bed onto carpet and it still completely died.

turns out thinkpads aren't as indestructable as people make them out to be.

what to do?

now obviously i wasn't gonna just leave it as a paperweight

first order of business was to reseat the ram and reset the cmos battery

doing that lead to nothing, so i tried disconnecting the ram entirely

and upon turning it on, there was no beep code to indicate the ram was disconnected

uh oh

thats not good

like, very not good

i tried disconnecting the trackpad and keyboard, the wifi module, anything i could easily disconnect.

plugged the power adapter back in and....

still nothing.

luckily, my partner was around to give me cuddles and calm me down so i didn't freak the fuck out over my 60 dollar paperweight.

and so i left it and kinda just forgot about it.

trying again

a few weeks had paassed since i first broke the laptop

i hadn't really touched it since a day or two after i broke it, and i was just fed up with looking at the husk of a laptop sitting on my desk.

it was incredibly depressing having to look at my dream laptop in this state every single day

so i decided to try again and fix it.

first order of business, starting from square 1 and trying to rediagnose everything.

i disconnected the ram, wifi module, trackpad, everything i tried before

lo and behold, it started beeping.

specifically, 1 3 3 1.

i immediately knew this was a ram issue, given i had to help a friend with his laptop making the same beep code a few years prior

so of course, i got overly excited, reinstalled the ram modules, and...

back to nothing.

however, disconnecting the ram again did cause it to do the beep code again, which gave me hope

then it occured to me, i never actually tried checking every ram module in every slot, meaning i could have just had bad luck and managed to accidently kill a ram stick or a ram slot.

so i tried switching the right module into the left slot, and it suddenly started working.

it's unfortunate that one of the ram slots is kinda just dead, but it is what it is.

it's always the simplest and most obvious solution lol

conclusions

if you break something, pretend it never existed and it will magically be fine again
