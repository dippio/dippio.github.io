hacking my telstra router(s)

if you are with telstra, you know how shit their routers are.

much to my dismay, i am still with them (ideally i'll have switched isps in the coming weeks but thats besides the point)

their routers are incredibly locked down, so i modded them to add some functions

f@st 5355

this was the first router that was "gifted" to me by telstra

it is a shit router, everything is so locked down, to the point where you can't even change the routers default DNS server

i think this is solely just for blocking piracy sites at the DNS level.

which is a really stupid way to go about that, but it makes it easy enough to bypass so i can't complain lol

but this is a huge issue, because the DNS servers telstra uses suck ass and go down constantly

literally 90% of the time i cant connect to the internet, ill check the routers error logs and it'll just be the dns server not propegating shit properly

luckily enough, this is an easy issue to fix since you can just run shell commands(?) from the browser console

yep, thats right, from the browser console, you can just enable ssh access lmao

and from there, sshing into the router and configuring things solves a lot of the issues i have with the router

telstra smart modem (gen 2)

my second foray with a telstra router, this time bought with my hard earned money.

(it was 10 australian dollars, which is equivalent to like 2 usd i think)

unfortunately for me, they upped the security on this router (thank god i guess? the other one was horrifically insecure)

i don't even remember why i wanted to mod this one, since i think it lets you use googles DNS servers, which probably would have sufficed.

i don't think i've even used any mods besides cpu and ram monitors since i install custom firmware lmao

regardless, it took me a long while to actually get it modded

i wanted to hit this fuckass router with a hammer so bad

given you're reading an article about something like modding routers, i'm assuming you actually do wanna hear the nitty gritty details

but i'm lazy and don't wanna write like 4 paragraphs about technical shit

so let's just pretend i'm leaving out the details for your sake, oki? :3

first attempt involved attempting to upload some modded firmware directly to the firmware banks and praying it would upload to the active bank, since only one of them can be written to externally

unfortunately, the router auto updated a few days prior, and switched which firmware bank it was using in the process, so my upload wrote to the non active bank

the only way to get around this is by forcing a bank switch by tricking the router into thinking the active firmware bank is unbootable

and at the time, there were no guides on how to do it on the smart modem by just power cycling it

so my last option was to take it apart and try to wire up cables to the serial pins

but after like 3 hours of taking it apart and inspecting the boards, i couldn't find them at all

and given the lack of proper documentation of the gen 2 online at the time, i just assumed continuing to search was a lost cause

(even now after like a whole year and getting a better understanding of the gen 2, i still don't even know if it has serial pins on the board lol)

so my last last option was basically to undervolt the shit out of it using a potentiometer

but that required me to do actual electrical work, which i didn't feel comfortable doing at the time, especially since i'd be risking shocking myself with 240 volts if i fucked up

so my last last last option was to wait

so i waited a whole year, praying that a new firmware version would be released so it would upgrade and switch to the writable bank

checking every day to see if new firmware was out

and eventually...

nothing lol, i think an update has been released since i modded the router, but i cant say for certain since i refuse to update

i'm just very lucky some kind soul uploaded a video of how to switch banks by power cycling lol

definitely not a fun or cool ending to my 11 months of misery, but it worked in the end, so i can't complain
